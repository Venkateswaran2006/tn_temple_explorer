Famous Temple Cities

Madurai

Ancient city ruled by Pandyas, known for the majestic Meenakshi Amman Temple.

Kumbakonam

The "Cambridge of South India," famous for its hundreds of temples and Mahamaham.

Mayiladuthurai

Legend has it that Goddess Parvati worshipped Lord Shiva here in the form of a peacock.

Rameswaram

Holy island where Lord Rama built a bridge to Lanka; famous for its long corridors.

Chidambaram

The center of the universe's cosmic dance, featuring the golden roofed Nataraja temple.

Thanjavur

Capital of the Great Cholas, home to the architectural marvel Big Temple (Brihadeeswarar).

Kanchipuram

Former Pallava capital, known as the "City of Thousand Temples" and silk sarees.

Tiruvannamalai

Home to the sacred Arunachala hill facing the Agni Lingam, representing fire.

Palani

Hilltop abode of Lord Murugan, famous for the Navapashanam idol and panchamirtham.

Srirangam

The foremost Divya Desam, located on an island formed by rivers Cauvery and Kollidam.

Mahabalipuram

Pallava port city renowned for its rock-cut monuments and Shore Temple.

Kanyakumari

The southern tip where three seas meet, dedicated to the virgin Goddess Kanyakumari.
